The Problem

How can we automate cherry-picking üçí when the wind is blowing, the branch is shaking and the cherries are trembling? This task is an example of grasping without rigid-surface support: the problem is inherently dynamic as any contact with the object might cause it to move and further disturb its movement. The difficulty becomes more prominent for fine manipulation of small objects, as the perception error and sensor noise dominate and grasps can hardly be precise.
